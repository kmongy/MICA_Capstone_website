Immigrants and refugees in the United States have always had stories to tell. Their stories have shaped and continue shaping history.

We often only hear about immigrant communities:

What if we could hear from immigrant and refugee communities instead?

21 oral histories were analyzed. tell their histories.

People, time, family, and children are just a few of the most frequently mentioned words in their stories.

Sentiment

Like any story, each oral history has peaks and valleys - positive and negative elements that comprise the whole.

The words used to describe their experiences were passed through a set of sentiment lexicons. The AFINN lexicon assigns a word a rating between -5 and 5, indicating a negative or positive sentiment.

Some oral histories were a little shorter than others when interviewed, but the words used to describe their experiences offer insight into their world.

When accounting for the stories as a whole, each person was given an average sentiment score.

All together, the average sentiment for all the stories combined is -0.035; a slightly negative sentiment.
